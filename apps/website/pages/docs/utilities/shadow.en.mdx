# Shadow

We provide a basic className `has-shadow` that can be used to define shadow on elements.

<CodeDemo>
  <div className="rounded-xl w-20 h-20 has-shadow"></div>
</CodeDemo>

And also provide a series of shadow classes to adjust the appearance of shadow.

> You can also combine these classes with your own discretion.

- `wider` `widest` `compact` control the radius of the shadow
- `bolder` `boldest` control the color saturation of the shadow
- `rainbow` a unique className to generate a shadow like rainbow

<CodeDemo>
  <div className="grid grid-cols-3 h-[50vh] gap-20 py-10 px-10 place-items-stretch place-content-stretch">
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow">default</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow wider">wider</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow widest">widest</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow compact">compact</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow compact bolder">compact bolder</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow compact boldest">compact boldest</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow bolder">bolder</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow boldest">boldest</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow boldest widest">boldest widest</div>
    <div className="rounded-xl has-shadow rainbow">rainbow</div>
  </div>
</CodeDemo>
